# truly-social

# Truly Social

> *"The opposite of love is not hate. It is indifference."*
> — Elie Wiesel

We have social media. We do not yet have truly social media.

This project is an open inquiry into that gap - built as working experiments, not arguments. Each kernel takes one philosophical claim about what human connection actually requires and makes it tangible through interaction. You use it, you feel the friction, you understand something that no essay could quite land.

---

## The thesis

Genuine social connection requires three things that current platforms do not provide:

**Meaning** - not engagement metrics, not reach, not likes. Viktor Frankl showed that human beings orient around meaning the way a plant orients toward light. Remove it and you get a restless emptiness that gets filled with conformism or tribalism. Current platforms offer meaning-shaped objects that do not carry meaning.

**Witness** - the discipline of truly seeing another person before responding. Elie Wiesel's work centres on this: to bear witness is a moral act. The scroll industrialises its opposite - indifference at scale, thousands of human worlds reduced to half a second before the thumb moves.

**I-Thou contact** - Martin Buber's distinction between treating a person as a subject (I-Thou) versus an object (I-It). Every design decision in current platforms converts persons into content. The architecture makes I-It the default and I-Thou the exception.

Iain McGilchrist adds the neurological frame: systems optimising for engagement will always favour the reductive, the fast, the certain - left-brain dominance at civilisational scale. The relational depth that makes connection real cannot survive that optimisation.

---

## What this is not

This is not a social network. It is not a startup. It is not a critique looking for an audience.

It is a collection of small, working proofs. Each one asks: *what if this one thing were required?* Then it lets you feel the answer.

## Kernels

Each kernel is an independent experiment. A philosophical claim. A simple working prototype. A prompt for what the experiment is measuring. Fork it, extend it, break it.

| Kernel | Claim | Status |
|--------|-------|--------|
| [witness](./kernels/witness) | You cannot respond to what you have not heard | `v0.1` |
| [delay](./kernels/delay) | Friction between thought and broadcast changes what gets said | `planned` |
| [meaning-check](./kernels/meaning-check) | Naming the purpose of a post changes whether you send it | `planned` |
| [tribal-mirror](./kernels/tribal-mirror) | Language shifts when identity is threatened - making it visible changes behaviour | `planned` |

---

## The structure of a kernel

Each kernel contains:

```
kernels/
  witness/
    README.md        # the philosophical claim and what the experiment measures
    index.html       # working prototype - no build step, runs in browser
    NOTES.md         # observations, forks, what people felt when they used it
```

The prototype is always simple. The README is always honest about what it is testing and what it cannot prove.

## Contributing

If you have a philosophical claim about human connection that can be made tangible through a simple interaction - open an issue. Describe the claim, the thinker behind it if there is one, and what the friction would feel like.

Good kernels are specific, fast to load, and leave the person who uses them slightly different than before.

## Background reading

These thinkers form the foundation. You do not need to have read them to contribute - but they are worth knowing.

- Viktor Frankl - *Man's Search for Meaning*
- Elie Wiesel - *Night*, and his essays on witness and indifference
- Martin Buber - *I and Thou*
- Iain McGilchrist - *The Master and His Emissary*
- Sherry Turkle - *Alone Together*
- Jonathan Haidt - *The Righteous Mind*

---

## A note on the name

We already have social media. The word *truly* carries the whole argument. What we built is called social. This project asks what the word would have to mean for the name to be accurate.
